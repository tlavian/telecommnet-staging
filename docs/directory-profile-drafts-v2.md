## RESEARCH FINDINGS

### How Attorneys Search Directories
1. **By specialty keyword** — "telecommunications", "network communications", "TCP/IP", "VoIP", "wireless"
2. **By case type** — "patent", "patent infringement", "PTAB", "ITC"
3. **By location** — California, then sort by relevance
4. **Scan behavior** — attorneys read the first 2-3 lines of each profile, then click "View Full Profile" if interested

### What Makes Attorneys Click (vs Skip)
1. **Specific numbers** — "70+ cases" beats "extensive experience"
2. **Named companies** — "Apple, Google, Cisco" beats "major technology companies"
3. **Named courts/venues** — "PTAB, ITC, federal courts" beats "various courts"
4. **Credentials up front** — Ph.D. UC Berkeley in first sentence
5. **Photo** — profiles with photos get 3-5x more clicks (per SEAK data)

### SEAK-Specific Rules (from SEAK's own optimization guide)
- **Do NOT use "expert witness" in keywords** — you're already in an expert witness directory
- **Use both General Specialty slots**
- **Optimize keywords for attorney search terms** — think case types and technology areas
- **Add cell phone number**
- **Use the Headline field** — larger text that stands out
- **Upload CV and photo**

### Critical Competitive Insight
Your closest competitor on SEAK (Dr. Istvan Jonyer) lists: "25+ court qualifications; deposed 5+ times; testified in court" and "50/50 plaintiff/defense experience." **Your numbers crush his** (70+ cases, 80+ depositions, 120+ patents vs his 20+ cases, ~5 depositions, 0 patents). But his profile communicates his strengths more clearly because he uses bullet points and direct statements.
